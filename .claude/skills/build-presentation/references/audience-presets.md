# Audience Presets

AI reference for audience-specific design rules. When building a presentation, identify the audience type first, then apply these quantified rules to guide component selection, content density, font sizes, and animation choices.

Every slide must pass the "So What" test: why should THIS audience care about THIS point? If you cannot answer, remove the slide.

## Quick Reference

| Audience | Slide Count | Max Words/Slide | Max Bullets | Font (Title/Body) | Animation |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| C-Suite/Board | 8-12 | 15-30 | 3 | 44pt / 28pt | Minimal |
| Stakeholder | 12-20 | 30-50 | 5 | 36pt / 22pt | None/Subtle |
| Technical | 15-30+ | 50-100 | 7+ | 36pt / 18pt | None |
| Sales/Pitch | 8-15 | 10-20 | 2 | 48pt / 28pt | Cinematic |
| Workshop | 20-40 | 40-80 | 6 | 36pt / 20pt | Step reveals |
| Internal | 5-10 | 30-60 | 5-7 | 32pt / 18pt | None |

## C-Suite / Board

- **Profile:** Executives, board members, C-level decision-makers. They make 35+ decisions daily and want conclusions, not analysis. Time is scarce -- they will interrupt with questions.
- **Slide count:** 8-12 slides for a 20-minute slot. Never exceed 15. Include appendix for backup detail.
- **Content density:** 15-30 words per slide. Maximum 3 bullets. 70% visual, 30% text. One insight per slide.
- **Font sizes:** Title 44-48pt, body 24-30pt, minimum 24pt anywhere. Large room readability.
- **Visual weight:** Big numbers, clean charts, executive dashboards. Abstract aspirational imagery.
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: title, metrics (1-3 big numbers), summary, framework, image-full-bleed. Avoid: text-heavy, card-grid. Use comparison for transformation stories.
- **Animation density:** Minimal. Only purposeful entrance animations. No bullet-by-bullet reveals.
- **Tone:** Formal, authoritative, strategic. Action titles required. Lead with the recommendation.

## Stakeholder Updates

- **Profile:** Project sponsors, department heads, cross-functional leads. They want to know: Are we on track? What changed? What do you need from me?
- **Slide count:** 12-20 slides for a 30-minute meeting. Consistent format across recurring updates.
- **Content density:** 30-50 words per slide. Maximum 5 bullets. 50% visual, 50% text. Status-oriented.
- **Font sizes:** Title 36-44pt, body 20-24pt, minimum 20pt. Standard meeting room.
- **Visual weight:** Status indicators (RAG), progress bars, milestone trackers. Budget vs actual comparisons.
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: metrics (3-5 with context), timeline (roadmaps), comparison (plan vs actual), two-column, summary. Use agenda for recurring structure. Avoid: quote, image-full-bleed.
- **Animation density:** None or subtle transitions. Consistency across updates matters more than visual flair.
- **Tone:** Professional, structured, metric-driven. Highlight deviations from plan. End with clear next steps and owners.

## Technical Deep-Dives

- **Profile:** Engineers, architects, analysts, domain experts. They want depth, precision, and methodology. They will evaluate feasibility and challenge assumptions.
- **Slide count:** 15-30+ slides acceptable. 2-4 minutes per slide is normal. Include appendix for deep detail.
- **Content density:** 50-100 words per slide. 7+ bullets acceptable. 40% visual, 60% text. Higher density expected.
- **Font sizes:** Title 32-40pt, body 16-20pt, minimum 14pt. Technical audiences accept smaller text for detail.
- **Visual weight:** Architecture diagrams, system flows, detailed data tables, code snippets. Substance over style.
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: text-heavy, two-column, framework (architecture), timeline (process), comparison. Use card-grid for feature breakdowns. Avoid: image-full-bleed, quote (unless expert endorsement).
- **Animation density:** None. All content visible immediately. Technical audiences prefer to scan at their own pace.
- **Tone:** Precise, evidence-based, detailed. Include edge cases, limitations, trade-offs. Show methodology.

## Sales / Pitch

- **Profile:** Prospects, investors, potential partners. Emotional connection drives decisions. Stories are 22x more memorable than facts alone. Position the audience as the hero.
- **Slide count:** 8-15 slides for a 20-minute pitch. Follow the 10/20/30 rule (Guy Kawasaki): 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt minimum.
- **Content density:** 10-20 words per slide. Maximum 2 bullets. 80% visual, 20% text. Presenter carries content verbally.
- **Font sizes:** Title 44-60pt, body 24-36pt, minimum 24pt. Dramatic, bold typography.
- **Visual weight:** Full-bleed photography, lifestyle imagery, visual metaphors. Bold brand colors. Social proof (logos, testimonials).
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: title (dramatic), image-full-bleed, metrics (1-2 hero numbers), quote (testimonials), contact (CTA), card-grid (offerings). Avoid: text-heavy, framework.
- **Animation density:** Cinematic, narrative. Purposeful entrance animations enhance storytelling. Use blurIn for hero moments, fadeUp for reveals.
- **Tone:** Persuasive, emotional, story-driven. Problem-solution-benefit arc. End with compelling CTA.

## Workshop / Training

- **Profile:** Participants learning a skill or process. Slides double as reference material and workbook pages. Must work as handouts when printed.
- **Slide count:** 20-40 slides for a half-day workshop. Include exercise instructions, recap slides, cheat sheets.
- **Content density:** 40-80 words per slide. Maximum 6 bullets. 50% visual, 50% text. Reference-oriented.
- **Font sizes:** Title 32-40pt, body 18-22pt, minimum 16pt. Must be legible as printed handouts.
- **Visual weight:** Icons, frameworks, templates, instructional diagrams. Color-coded by activity type (instruction, exercise, reference, discussion).
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: text-heavy (instructions), card-grid (frameworks), two-column (exercise+reference), summary (recap), timeline (process steps), section-break (progress markers). Avoid: image-full-bleed, quote.
- **Animation density:** Step-by-step reveals for instructions. Progressive disclosure for exercises. Fragment animations useful here.
- **Tone:** Encouraging, interactive, supportive. Numbered steps for activities. Include timing cues in speaker notes.

## Internal / All-Hands

- **Profile:** Your own team. They already have context -- do not re-establish it. Action-oriented, efficient. Possibly asynchronous (read-ahead).
- **Slide count:** 5-10 slides for a 10-minute update. Can be more raw/draft-quality than external decks.
- **Content density:** 30-60 words per slide. 5-7 bullets fine. 30% visual, 70% text. Dense and scannable.
- **Font sizes:** Title 32-36pt, body 16-20pt, minimum 14pt. Informal, dense is acceptable.
- **Visual weight:** Minimal design polish. Screenshots, quick mockups, tables. Whatever communicates fastest.
- **Component selection bias:** Prefer: text-heavy (updates), metrics (team KPIs), two-column, summary (action items), comparison. Use agenda for meeting structure. Avoid: image-full-bleed, quote, contact.
- **Animation density:** None. All content visible immediately. Speed matters.
- **Tone:** Casual, direct, shorthand. Use team-specific terminology. Focus on blockers, decisions, next actions with owners and deadlines.
